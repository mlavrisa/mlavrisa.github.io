About Tidal Productivity App

Meet Tidal — an all-in-one productivity platform that serves as your personal accountability buddy, to-do manager, habit tracker, and journaling companion. Tidal harnesses the power of an encouraging language model that greets you each morning, checks in on your progress, and offers helpful advice and thoughtful reframing when setbacks occur. By integrating your habits list, daily tasks, and personal bio, Tidal tailors its guidance to what matters most to you.

Every morning, Tidal greets you and prompts a reflective discussion about your previous day's progress and today's plans. It even transforms your daily chat into a journal entry and compiles weekly and monthly summaries for you to review and edit. This comprehensive approach encourages a solid morning routine and continuous personal growth, helping you achieve your goals.

The project is made using the eel framework, combining python, and vanilla javascript and css. I make streaming calls to an LLM endpoint for the chat agent, and store all of the tasks, habits, messages and journal entries in a sqlite database.

The main interface, illustrated above, is split into two key sections. On the left, you have an interactive LLM chat window where Tidal listens and responds with encouragement. On the right, a task list helps you plan your day. This task list is designed with several interactive features:

  • A checkbox to mark tasks as complete.
  • A toggle to indicate whether you plan to work on that task today.
  • A snooze button to defer tasks when needed.
  • An Eisenhower matrix quadrant indicator to help prioritize tasks by urgency and importance.
  • A description of the task

  • A due date display to keep your schedule organized.

In addition to managing your tasks, Tidal includes a robust habits tracker to help you build and maintain positive routines. The habits list, shown in the above image, has the following functionality:

  • A radio button that lets you select the primary habit you want to focus on.
  • An indicator showing which habits you've already established.
  • Descriptions for each habit
  • A field specifying when you plan to perform them.
  • The minimal action required to count as progress.
  • An explanation of why each habit is important, connecting your daily actions to your broader goals.

Despite being a relatively simple desktop-only app, I have found it to be an incredibly helpful tool for my own productivity and self-improvement path. I use it every day and find it to be a great way to stay focused and motivated. I plan to beta test it with some friends and produce a web version and mobile app in the future.
